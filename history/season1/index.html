The Scarborough Table Tennis Ranking

Season 1: The Nautilus Crescent Season

Back to Current Season

Season 1 got off to a hotly contested start in September 2016. Nine different countries were represented in an overwhelmingly British affair. Tournament host Benjamin 'Lex' Letter (neé Goodchild) came flying out of the blocks with five wins in a row. He would never lose the top spot on the table throughout the entire season!

Despite his fast start young Benjamin was not able to draw inspiration from Arsène Wenger's 2003-04 Arsenal side and remain unbeaten throughout the season. He suffered his first defeat that very same month, and in fact even lost two games in a row at the end of January 2017.

It was around this time that our Champion began to experience his first real challenger; a punchy young chap by the name of Judd Schmeissing. Schmeissing proved to be the only athlete able to seriously threaten the top of the table, but as the season drew to a close Benjamin rediscovered some of his early-season form. He finished strong through July, August and September 2017 with an unbeaten streak of 10 games on the trot to comfortably take the title. Schmeissing finished a respectable second but will know that he has a long way to go in Season 2.

Honourable mentions, then, to the other three players to finish Season 1 with a winning record.

Justin Fifield only played twice but won both games... Will he manage to break the tyrannical reign of Lex and give us a different winner in Season 2?

In the Ladies contest Appin Williamson comfortably saw off her competition, finishing an impressive fourth overall and even beating one of the boys en route (although that was the wooden spoon, so let's not get too carried away).

Lastly, Daniel Orr also scrapped to the end of the season in the black. Will his compact style be well suited to the smaller pitch rumoured to host Season 2?
